GPT-4o ("omni") was released with impressive demos of real-time voice conversation and image understanding. But what actually changed architecturally, and where does the model still struggle?

## The Architecture Shift: End-to-End Multimodality

Previous GPT-4 variants used a modular pipeline:
- **Audio** → Whisper (ASR) → text → GPT-4 → text → TTS → **audio**

GPT-4o replaces this with a single model that processes audio, vision, and text tokens in a unified transformer. This eliminates information loss at conversion boundaries and dramatically reduces latency.

The key difference is the **token vocabulary**: GPT-4o likely uses a multi-codebook approach where audio is discretized into tokens that live in the same embedding space as text. This is similar to AudioPaLM and SpeechGPT but at unprecedented scale.

## What This Means in Practice

**Better prosody**: Because the model sees text and audio jointly, it can generate speech with appropriate emotion and rhythm rather than flat TTS output. The demo of it laughing authentically wasn't faked.

**Faster response**: The old pipeline introduced ~400ms of inherent latency from the two conversions. The native audio model responds in ~200ms, approaching human conversation pace.

**Implicit audio information**: The model can now "hear" background sounds, accents, emotional tone — information that was completely lost when converting to a transcript first.

## Where It Still Struggles

Despite the improvements, consistent failure modes remain:

**1. Long-context reasoning**: Even with 128k tokens, performance degrades significantly past ~30k tokens on tasks requiring integration of information across the full window.

**2. Calibration**: The model is often confident when wrong. It doesn't know what it doesn't know — and post-training RLHF makes this worse, not better, by rewarding confident-sounding outputs.

**3. Math and code**: GPT-4o is slightly *worse* than GPT-4 Turbo on competitive math benchmarks. The multimodal training appears to have traded some reasoning depth for breadth.

<Callout title="The Honesty Gap">
The most important benchmark isn't MMLU or HumanEval — it's how often the model says "I don't know" when it should. No model does this well enough for high-stakes applications.
</Callout>

## The Real Competition

GPT-4o's real competition isn't other GPT variants — it's Claude 3.5 Sonnet (better on coding and analysis) and Gemini 1.5 Pro (much longer effective context window).

The multimodal unification is genuinely impressive engineering, but for most text-only workloads, the improvement over GPT-4 Turbo is incremental. The voice experience is the real breakthrough — it's the first time talking to an LLM has felt *natural*.
